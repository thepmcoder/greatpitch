# Video Production Principles (Extracted from C2D Build)

> **71 principles** across 9 categories, distilled from iterative pitch video creation.
> These are project-independent — applicable to any strategic pitch video.
> Full source: C2D/Video-Production-Principles.md

## Most Critical Principles (Top 10)

### 1. The Script Is the Timeline
Word anchors bind visuals to what is being said, not when. Re-record narration, change voice, edit pacing — visuals still sync. This is the foundational principle.

### 2. On-Screen Text Is a Subset, Not a Paraphrase (N1)
Same keywords as narration, just shorter. "The customer need is real" on screen when the narrator says "the customer need is real — everyday, people see something." Different phrasing = cognitive dissonance.

### 3. Audio-Position Triggers, Never Wall-Clock Timers (B-series)
Use `requestAnimationFrame` polling `audio.currentTime`. Never `setTimeout`. Wall-clock drifts. Audio-position cannot drift.

### 4. Progressive Reveal — Beat by Beat (V1)
Never show all content at once. Each element appears when the narrator reaches that point. Keeps eyes tracking with narration.

### 5. Spoken Narration Must Be Self-Contained Per Sentence (N2)
Every sentence must land on its own when heard in isolation. Video is heard once — no re-reads.

### 6. Don't Oversell — Present Hypotheses as Hypotheses (N4)
"This is still a hypothesis" is more credible than "This will succeed." Leadership audiences smell overselling instantly.

### 7. Two-Touch Approach for New Concepts (N6)
Plant the fact first, draw the conclusion later in a different section. The audience connects the dots — making the conclusion feel earned, not pushed.

### 8. Provocative Questions Earn Attention (N7)
"Can we be correct all the time?" > "We don't need to be correct every time." Questions make the audience lean in.

### 9. End with a Concrete Ask, Not an Emotional Plea (N9)
"Let's plan experiments, set timelines, and discuss" > "We believe this is the future."

### 10. Anchor Words Early in Sentences
Anchoring to a word at 80% of a sentence means the visual appears when the audience has already processed it. Anchor to words in the first 60% of each sentence.
