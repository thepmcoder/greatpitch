# Test Scenario P03: FreshLocal

## Product Brief

**Company:** FreshLocal (Seed, $1.5M raised)
**Product:** Farm-to-door marketplace connecting local farms with urban consumers
**Stage:** Live in 2 cities (Austin, Portland), 340 farms, 8,200 consumers

**The Problem:**
Average vegetable travels 1,500 miles to reach a plate. Farmers lose 40% of revenue to distributors and retailers. Consumers want local fresh produce but farmers' markets are inconvenient (limited hours, weather-dependent). CSA boxes solve freshness but not choice — you get what the farm grows, not what you want.

**The Solution:**
Marketplace where consumers browse real-time inventory from local farms (within 50 miles), build custom orders, get next-day delivery. Farms upload what they harvested today. Platform handles logistics (shared cold-chain trucks, milk-run routes), payment, and communication.

**Key differentiator:** Produce is never warehoused. Farm → truck → door in under 24 hours. Average shelf age at delivery: 14 hours (vs 5-7 days for grocery stores).

**Traction:**
- 340 farms, 8,200 consumers, 2 cities
- Average order: $47 (vs $32 grocery delivery)
- Monthly repeat rate: 64%
- Farm revenue increase: avg 35% vs wholesale
- Consumer NPS: +61
- Unit economics: -$3.20/order, trending breakeven at 15K orders/month/city

**The Ask:**
$6M Series A to expand to 5 more cities and reach logistics breakeven.

**Audience:** Consumer/marketplace VCs, 20-minute presentation.
