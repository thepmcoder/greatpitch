# Test Scenario P04: StudyBuddy

## Product Brief

**Company:** StudyBuddy (Pre-seed, $500K angel round)
**Product:** AI tutor for K-12 students that adapts to individual learning pace
**Stage:** Beta with 1,200 students across 8 schools

**The Problem:**
One teacher, thirty students, thirty different levels. Teachers know differentiated instruction works — students learn 40% faster when content matches their level. But no teacher can run 30 personalized lesson plans simultaneously. After-school tutoring costs $40-80/hour, pricing out most families. Khan Academy helps but has no feedback loop — it doesn't know when a student is confused, frustrated, or ready to advance.

**The Solution:**
An AI tutor that sits alongside the student during homework and practice. It watches how they solve problems — not just right/wrong, but HOW they work through it. When a student makes a conceptual error (not a typo), it doesn't give the answer. It asks a question that leads them back to the concept they missed. Like a patient human tutor, but available at 9 PM on a Tuesday.

**Key features:**
- Covers math (grades 3-8), expanding to science
- Detects misconceptions from work patterns, not just answers
- Explains in multiple ways (visual, verbal, analogy) until one clicks
- Weekly parent reports showing conceptual progress, not just scores
- Teacher dashboard showing class-wide misconception patterns

**Traction:**
- 1,200 students across 8 schools (mix of suburban and Title I)
- Students using StudyBuddy 4+ times/week show 0.6 grade-level improvement in 3 months
- Teacher time spent on remediation: down 35%
- Student self-reported confidence in math: +28 percentile points
- Parent NPS: +67
- Cost: $8/student/month (vs $40-80/hr for human tutoring)

**The Ask:**
$3M seed round to expand to 50 schools and add science curriculum.

**Audience:** EdTech investors, 12-minute presentation slot.
